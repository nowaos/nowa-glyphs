App Icon Structure

Rules enforced by scripts/check_apps.rb and autofixes/reapply_shadows.rb

Every app icon is drawn on a 64×64 px canvas. The visible shape occupies a 55×55 rect inset 4.5 px from each edge — that border is reserved exclusively for the drop shadow and must never contain artwork. The SVG root must not carry any transform attribute; all sizing is done by editing paths directly, never by scaling the root element.

Two background shapes are permitted. The rounded square (rx="10") is the standard form used by the vast majority of app icons. The circle (rx="27.5") exists for apps whose icon is inherently circular — think Spotify or Google Chrome. Rather than inscribing their round shape inside a square background, the bg itself becomes the circle, preserving the app's original visual identity. No other rx value is valid.

SVG Structure

The listing below is the complete allowed content at SVG root — nothing else may appear at that level. No extra <path>, <rect>, or bare gradient elements outside <defs>. The order also reflects the paint stack: shadow first, background on top, artwork above that. These rules are enforced by check_apps.rb.

IDElementDescription
ds<g>Drop shadow — 8 gradient segments at opacity 0.25. Auto-managed by reapply_shadows.rb
bg<rect>Background shape — carries the gradient fill color
art<g>Icon artwork — paths, shapes, glyphs placed over the background
em<g>Emblem overlay — optional badge (e.g. alert, lock, plus)
<svg viewBox="0 0 64 64" width="64" height="64">
  <defs> … </defs>  ← gradients, filters, clip paths
  <g id="ds">…</g>
  <rect id="bg" x="4.5" y="4.5" width="55" height="55" rx="10" fill="…"/>
  <g id="art">…</g>
  <!-- <g id="em">…</g> optional -->
</svg>

No loose definitions at root

Gradients, filters, clip paths, and symbols must live inside <defs>. A bare <linearGradient> or <filter> placed directly at SVG root is treated as an unrecognized element and will cause check_apps.rb to reject the file.

Never edit ds manually

The drop shadow group is regenerated automatically by reapply_shadows.rb from the canonical template in src/apps/templates/. Any manual edits will be overwritten on the next script run. The square vs. round variant is chosen based on the rx value of bg.

em is optional

<g id="em"> serves two related purposes: emblem badges (a lock, a plus, an alert) and decorative elements that float above the artwork. The key distinction from art is that em content is allowed to overflow the background bounds slightly — for example, a pencil whose tip extends just beyond the edge of the paper. The art group must always stay fully contained within bg. Use good judgment: a small intentional overflow adds dynamism; abusing it breaks the visual consistency of the theme.
